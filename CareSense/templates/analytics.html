Random Forest (50%)

Highest weight — best cross-validation. Excels at non-linear symptom interactions and is robust to correlated features. 100 estimators, captures complex co-occurrence patterns.

SVM (35%)

Strong on high-dimensional sparse binary features. The RBF kernel maps symptom vectors to a higher-dimensional space, finding non-linear decision boundaries between diseases.

Naive Bayes (15%)

Fast probabilistic classifier. Adds calibrated probability diversity to the ensemble. Despite the independence assumption, performs well given severity-weighted features reduce correlated noise.
