Important Medical Disclaimer

CareSense is an educational and research tool only. It does not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. All predictions are probabilistic and based on a limited training dataset. Never use this system as a substitute for professional medical care. Always consult a licensed physician before making any health decisions.

{# ══ WHAT IT DOES ══════════════════════════════════════════════ #}

What CareSense Does

Symptom Analysis

Accepts up to 17 clinical symptoms, severity-weighted on a 1–7 scale. Chest pain carries far more weight than mild itching — reflecting real clinical priority in the prediction.

Ensemble Voting

Random Forest (50%), SVM (35%), and Naive Bayes (15%) each vote. Their probability vectors are averaged, producing a more reliable diagnosis than any single model alone.

Differential Diagnosis

The top 5 candidate diseases are shown with confidence bars. Each expands to reveal which of your symptoms strongly, moderately, or weakly support that diagnosis — and which key symptoms you didn't report.

Emergency Detection

8 critical symptom combinations — including the cardiac triad, stroke FAST symptoms, and anaphylaxis indicators — trigger an immediate emergency alert before results are shown.

Duration Weighting

Each symptom carries a duration selector: Hours (0.6×), Days (1.0×), Weeks (1.3×), Months+ (1.6×). A cough you've had for months weighs more than one from this morning — because clinically, it should.

Body Map Selection

An interactive SVG body silhouette lets you tap any region — Head, Chest, Abdomen, Legs, Skin, Urinary, and more — to browse and select region-specific symptoms visually rather than typing them.

Risk Factor Profiling

Age group (Under 18 → 70+), Diabetic, Hypertensive, and Smoker flags apply post-ensemble probability boosts. A diabetic user sees a higher weighting for Diabetes; a smoker for Bronchial Asthma.

Re-Diagnose Flow

From any result page, tap "Add Symptoms" to open the re-analyse overlay. Your existing symptoms are locked in; add new ones with durations and re-run — a purple badge marks the refined result.

Random Forest, SVM, and Naive Bayes ensemble — all from sklearn's unified API with soft-voting probability averaging and cross-validation.

{# ══ HOW A PREDICTION IS MADE ══════════════════════════════════ #}

How a Prediction is Made

1

Symptom Input, Duration & Body Map

Symptoms are entered via search, quick chips, or the SVG body map. Each symptom gets a duration selector (Hours / Days / Weeks / Months+) which becomes a multiplier on that symptom's feature value, capturing clinical chronicity before the model even sees the data.

2

Severity-Weighted Feature Vector

Each symptom is looked up in the severity weight table (weights 1–7). Its final feature value is (weight / max_weight) × duration_multiplier, producing a 132-dimensional input vector where both clinical severity and symptom chronicity are encoded.

3

Emergency Pre-Check

Before prediction, 8 critical symptom combinations are tested — cardiac triad (chest pain + breathlessness + sweating), stroke FAST indicators, anaphylaxis, and more. If any match, a full-screen emergency alert fires immediately with a direct 112 call button.

4

Ensemble Soft Voting

The input vector is passed to all three models. Each returns a 41-class probability vector. These are combined as 0.50 × RF + 0.35 × SVM + 0.15 × NB. The class with the highest weighted average probability is the prediction.

5

Risk Factor Probability Boost

Post-ensemble, if risk factors are set, relevant disease probabilities are multiplied by a boost factor (e.g. Diabetes +60% for diabetic users, Bronchial Asthma +50% for smokers). The top-5 list is then re-sorted with these adjusted scores capped at 99.9%.

6

Dynamic Confidence Threshold

Your total severity score determines the minimum confidence required. Low-severity scores require 30%; critical scores require up to 70%. If confidence falls below threshold, a doctor consultation advisory is shown prominently alongside the result.

7

Follow-Up Validation

Up to 3 key diagnostic symptoms for the predicted disease are surfaced as checkbox questions. Confirmed symptoms are merged, durations assigned, and the full pipeline re-runs — results validated this way display a green "Validated via follow-up" badge.

8

Differential Explanation & Re-Diagnose

Each top-5 disease shows which of your symptoms are strong (≥70% training frequency), moderate (30–69%), or weak matches, plus key symptoms you didn't report. The "Add Symptoms" button on the result page lets you extend the symptom list and re-run the full pipeline with a re-analyse badge on the new result.

{# ══ DATASET ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════ #}

Training Dataset

CareSense is trained on the Disease Prediction Using Machine Learning dataset — a widely-used benchmark containing symptom–disease mappings across 41 conditions and 4,920 training records. Symptom severity weights (1–7) are drawn from a separate clinical severity reference file with 133 entries.

The ensemble combines Random Forest (best cross-validation accuracy), SVM (strong on high-dimensional sparse features), and Naive Bayes (fast, probabilistic, good on symptom co-occurrence patterns). Their complementary strengths produce a more reliable final output than any single model.

Disease information — descriptions, medications, precautions, diets, and workout recommendations — is sourced from supplementary CSV reference files and surfaced post-prediction for every result.

{# ══ LIMITATIONS ═══════════════════════════════════════════════ #}

Known Limitations

Limited Training Data

4,920 records across 41 diseases. Real-world disease prevalence, demographics, and symptom variability are far more complex than any single benchmark dataset can capture.

No Physical Examination

CareSense cannot measure vitals, examine a patient, or order tests. Diagnoses that require physical signs, lab results, or imaging are outside its scope entirely.

Binary Symptom Encoding

Symptoms are treated as present or absent. Intensity, location specificity, and associated signs that a clinician would assess are not captured beyond severity weighting and duration.

Geographic & Demographic Bias

The training dataset reflects a specific epidemiological context. Disease prevalence differs globally. Risk factor boosts are intentionally conservative estimates, not clinical-grade adjustments.

Health Guidance

Safe Medication Use

  • Always follow prescribed dosage — never self-adjust
  • Complete the full course even when symptoms improve
  • Inform your doctor of all medications including supplements
  • Never share prescription medicine with others
  • Store medicines as directed — heat and humidity degrade them

When to See a Doctor

  • Symptoms lasting more than 3 days without improvement
  • Fever above 39.4°C (103°F) in adults
  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing — seek emergency care immediately
  • Any sudden severe or rapidly worsening symptoms
  • When CareSense shows a "Consultation Recommended" advisory

Preventive Health

  • Schedule annual health check-ups and screenings
  • Stay up-to-date with recommended vaccinations
  • Exercise at least 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly
  • Aim for 7–9 hours of quality sleep per night
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in whole foods and fibre

Understanding AI Health Tools

  • AI analyses patterns — it does not examine patients
  • Results are probabilistic, not definitive diagnoses
  • Use CareSense to prepare for — not replace — a doctor visit
  • Always discuss AI results with your physician
  • A high confidence score is not a guarantee of accuracy
